Biography

I am a postdoctoral associate at The Graham Lab at Georgia Institute of Technology.

Experience

 
 
 
 
 

Postdoctoral Fellow

George W. Woodruff School of Mechanical Engineering,
Georgia Institute of Technology

2018 – Present Atlanta, GA
Advisor: Prof. Samuel Graham

  • Developed and applied theoretical and numerical methods (e.g. full first Brillouin zone Landauer approach, non-equilibrium molecular dynamics (NEMD), density functional theory (DFT), etc.) to calculate thermal properties of materials and at interfaces to optimize thermal management design of power electronic devices.
  • Demonstrated different mechanisms (e.g. phonon bridge layer, patterned interface, etc.) to enhance the thermal transport across interfaces with atomistic scale modeling methods and experiments for efficient heat dissipation of devices.
  • Understood the effects of defects, nanostructures, growth methods, energy carrier coupling (e.g. electron-phonon) on thermal properties of materials and interfaces from simulation and experiments to guide the design of materials and devices satisfying different requirements.
 
 
 
 
 

Research Assistant

School of Mechanical Engineering,
Purdue University

2012 – 2018 West Lafayette, IN
Advisor: Prof. Xiulin Ruan and Prof. Timothy Fisher

  • Developed a network model to predict the macroscale thermal conductivity of 3D hierarchical carbon-based architectures with minimal atomistic-scale modeling to capture bulk property without losing the nano-features with reasonable computational cost.
  • Found a new energy transport channel through phonon polarization conversion (e.g. from longitudinal acoustic to transverse acoustic) at dimensionally mismatched interfaces with phonon wave-packet method.
  • Identified three mechanisms which lead to thermal resistances across the CNT-graphene junction and decomposed the thermal boundary resistance (TBR) to different mechanisms.
  • Developed modal non-equilibrium Landauer approach to capture the local non-equilibrium at the interface and significantly increased the accuracy of thermal boundary conductance (TBC) modeling.
  • Designed a pore-aligned pillared-graphene structure with inter-layer defects to enhance the thermal transport in multilayer CNT-graphene networks.
